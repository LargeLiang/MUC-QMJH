# 面向多模态大语言模型的人类偏好对齐研究

## 摘要

多模态大语言模型（MLLM）能够根据用户提供的文本指令及视觉输入，以零样本或少样本的推理方式灵活地完成各类视觉语言任务，为实现通用人工智能提供了一条可行的路径。然而，这些模型在实际应用中仍面临显著挑战，其中最为关键的就是幻觉问题。具体而言，模型在生成过程中可能产生看似合理，但实际与输入图像不一致的内容，这种不可靠的输出严重制约了模型的实际应用价值。已经有很多研究针对幻觉缓解进行展开，提出的方法可以分为四类：模型结构优化、训练时优化、推理时干预及输出后处理。这些方法虽然有效，但仍存在以下问题：（1）对于训练时优化的方法，现有的方法都需要使用人工或专家模型来标注高质量的训练数据，成本很高。且由于多模态大语言模型视觉模块的能力是有限的，强制其拟合到完美的标注响应可能会带来额外的幻觉。（2）对于推理时干预的方法，现有的方法没有充分地缓解模型对视觉和文本注意力的不平衡问题。针对当前多模态大语言模型幻觉缓解方法的局限性，本文展开了深入研究，并提出了两种幻觉缓解方法，分别通过偏好训练和推理干预对模型幻觉进行改善。

首先，本文提出了一种自动化的偏好数据构建方法。该方法通过对输入图像施加噪声，激发模型对文本先验的依赖，从而引发其内部的幻觉模式，生成非首选响应。同时，模型的原始输出被视为首选响应，以构建成对的偏好数据。接着，生成的偏好数据将通过CLIP 校验模块进行筛查，以提高数据质量。然后，采用直接偏好优化算法对模型进行训练，通过学习不同响应间细粒度的幻觉差异，使模型能够避免生成幻觉内容，而不仅仅是简单地拟合完美的标注响应。随后，本文将偏好学习中首选响应与非首选响应间“对比”的思路扩展到模型推理阶段，提出了一种以图像为中心的对比解码方法，用以平衡多模态大语言模型对视觉和文本信息的关注。具体来说，该方法通过干预模型对视觉标记的注意力权重来构建视觉强化分布，同时通过扰动输入图像来构建语言强化分布。然后，通过联合对比解码调整模型的原始输出分布，在增强视觉信息的同时减轻文本偏差，将模型引导向更基于图像的可靠方向。广泛的实验结果表明，本文提出的方法具有良好的幻觉缓解能力。

关键词：多模态大语言模型，幻觉缓解，偏好学习，对比解码

## ABSTRACT

Multimodal large language models can flexibly complete various vision-language tasks through zero-shot or few-shot reasoning based on textual instructions and visual inputs provided by users, offering a promising pathway toward achieving artificial general intelligence. However, these models still face significant challenges in practical applications, with the hallucination problem being one of the most critical. Specifically, during the generation process, the model may produce content that seems reasonable but is inconsistent with the input image. This unreliable output severely limits the practical value of the model. A great deal of research has been conducted to address hallucinations, and the proposed methods can be categorized into three types: optimization during training, intervention during inference, and post-output processing. While these methods are effective, they still have the following issues: (1) For training-based optimization methods, existing approaches require the use of manually or expert-labeled high-quality training data, which is costly. Moreover, because the vision module of multimodal large language models has limitations, forcing the model to perfectly fit the labeled responses may lead to additional hallucinations. (2) For inference-based intervention methods, current approaches do not fully alleviate the imbalance in the model’s attention to visual and textual information.To address the limitations of current hallucination mitigation methods in multimodal large language models, this thesis conducts in-depth research and proposes two hallucination mitigation methods, which improve model hallucinations through preference training and inference intervention.

First, this thesis introduces an automated preference data construction method, which applies noise to the input image, triggering the model’s reliance on textual priors and inducing its internal hallucination patterns, thereby generating non-preferred responses, while the model’s original output is considered as the preferred response to construct paired preference data. Secondly, the generated preference data is screened by the CLIP verification module to improve its quality. Finally, a direct preference optimization algorithm is used to train the model, enabling it to learn fine-grained hallucination differences between responses and avoid generating hallucinated content, rather than simply fitting to perfect labeled responses. Next, the thesis applies the idea of ”contrast” between preferred and non-preferred responses in preference learning to the model’s inference process, proposing an image-centered contrastive decoding method to balance the attention of multimodal large language models on visual and textual information. Specifically, this method constructs a visually enhanced distribution by intervening in the attention values of visual tokens, while constructing a linguistically enhanced distribution by perturbing the input image. Finally, by jointly decoding with contrast, the model’s original output distribution is modified to enhance visual information while reducing textual bias, guiding the model toward a more image-based and reliable direction. Extensive experimental results show that the proposed method has good hallucination mitigation capability.

Keywords: Multimodal Large Language Models, Hallucination Mitigation, Preference Learning, Contrastive Decoding
