# 多目标对齐大语言模型的强化学习策略研究

## 摘要

近年来，以GPT 为代表的大语言模型在自然语言处理领域取得了显著进展。然而，要确保其在应用中可靠又实用，核心在于使其行为能同时满足多个、甚至相互冲突的人类期望（如信息准确性、内容安全性等）。当前，在实现这种多目标对齐的过程中，现有方法在奖励信号的精确表征、多目标的有效权衡以及对齐过程的灵活控制等方面仍面临挑战，这已成为制约模型在关键领域深度应用的核心瓶颈。基于上述背景本文提出一种创新的对齐策略，集中体现为以下三点核心贡献：

（1）本文提出并实现了一种基于多维度绝对评分和混合专家机制的多目标奖励模型。方法通过引入多维度绝对评分机制，克服了传统成对比较方法带来的信息损失；并创新性地集成了混合专家架构，通过门控网络实现了对不同奖励维度重要性的动态、上下文感知评估与聚合，同时有效缓解了冗余性偏见。模型的设计旨在为后续的强化学习优化提供一个高质量、可解释、且更精确的评价基础。

（2）构建并验证了一个迭代式多目标可控对齐框架。该框架有机整合了奖励模型提供的精细化多目标奖励信号、迭代式同策略偏好采样机制以及基于可控偏好优化的算法。该框架通过一个从精确评价到有效优化再到持续改进的完整流程，旨在实现对模型多目标对齐程度和可控性的系统性提升。

（3）通过在十亿参数级别的大语言模型上进行的系统性实验，证实了上述对齐框架构成的协同对齐策略在提升模型多目标对齐综合性能方面的显著优势。实验结果表明：多目标奖励模型在RewardBench 和RMB 基准上的得分（分别为89.2和0.650）均优于传统基线。在模型对齐效果层面，经所提对齐框架训练的模型在AlpacaEval 上的长度控制胜率达到40.8，在MT-Bench 上的平均分达到8.72，全面超越了基线方法。这些量化的性能提升，充分证明了该策略在增强模型多目标对齐能力方面的有效性，及其在赋能关键落地应用场景方面的巨大潜力。

综上所述，本文通过多目标奖励模型和可控对齐框架的协同创新，为解决大语言模型在多样化应用场景下实现高质量多目标对齐所面临的挑战提供了一套高效、灵活、可解释且经过充分验证的解决方案。研究成果不仅丰富了多目标强化学习对齐的理论与实践，也为推动人工智能更安全、更可靠、更智能地服务于社会各领域具有重要的学术价值和实际应用前景。

关键词：自然语言处理，大语言模型，人工智能对齐，强化学习，多目标优化

## ABSTRACT

In recent years, Large Language Models (LLMs), exemplified by GPT, haveachieved significant advancements in the field of Natural Language Processing. However,ensuring their reliability and practicality in real-world applications hinges onaligning their behavior to simultaneously satisfy multiple, and often conflicting, human expectations (e.g., informational accuracy, content safety). Currently, in the process of achieving such multi-objective alignment, existing methods face significant challenges in the precise representation of reward signals, the effective trade-off among multiple objectives, and the flexible control of the alignment process. These challenges have become a core bottleneck constraining the deep application of LLMs in critical domains. Against this backdrop, this thesis proposes an innovative alignment strategy, centered on
the following three core contributions:

(1) This thesis proposes and implements a novel Multi-Objective Reward Model (MORM) based on multi-dimensional absolute scoring and a Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) mechanism. The method overcomes the information loss inherent in traditional pairwise comparison approaches by introducing a multi-dimensional absolute scoring mechanism. It innovatively integrates an MoE architecture to enable dynamic, context-aware assessment and aggregation of the importance of different reward dimensions via a gating network, while also effectively mitigating verbosity bias. Experimental results demonstrate that, compared to baseline methods, this model exhibits significant advantages in accurately evaluating and balancing multi-dimensional preferences, thereby providing a highquality evaluation foundation for subsequent reinforcement learning.

(2) An iterative, multi-objective, controllable alignment framework (IMOCA) is constructed and validated. This framework organically integrates the fine-grained multi-objective reward signals from our proposed MORM, an iterative on-policy preference sampling mechanism to enhance data quality and model adaptability, and an algorithm based on Controllable Preference Optimization (CPO) to achieve explicit and flexible control over multiple objectives via external preference condition instructions. The framework establishes a complete optimization pipeline, proceeding from precise evaluation to effective optimization and continuous improvement. Experiments show that models trained with this framework significantly outperform baselines on multiple alignment evaluation benchmarks, effectively enhancing the model’s degree of multi-objective alignment and controllability.

(3) Systematic experiments conducted on a billion-parameter-scale Large Language Model (LLM) have confirmed the significant advantages of the synergistic alignment strategy constituted by the aforementioned alignment framework in enhancing the model’s comprehensive multi-objective alignment performance. The experimental results demonstrate that: at the reward model level, our Multi-Objective Reward Model achieved scores of 89.2 on RewardBench and 0.650 on the RMB benchmark, both outperforming traditional baselines. At the model alignment efficacy level, the model trained with the proposed alignment framework achieved a length-controlled win-rate of 40.8 on AlpacaEval and an average score of 8.72 on MT-Bench, comprehensively surpassing baseline methods.These quantified performance enhancements fully substantiate the effectiveness of this strategy in improving the model’s multi-objective alignment capabilities and reveal its substantial potential for empowering key real-world application scenarios.

In summary, through the synergistic innovation of the multi-objective reward model and the controllable alignment framework, this thesis provides an efficient, flexible, interpretable, and thoroughly validated solution to the problem of multi-objective alignment for LLMs in diverse application scenarios. The research findings not only enrich the theory and practice of multi-objective reinforcement learning alignment but also hold significant academic value and practical prospects for advancing Artificial Intelligence to serve various societal sectors more safely, reliably, and intelligently. 

Keywords: Natural Language Processing, Large Language Model, Artificial Intelligence Alignment, Reinforcement Learning, Multi-Objective Optimization
